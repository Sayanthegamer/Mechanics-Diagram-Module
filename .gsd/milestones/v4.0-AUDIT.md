## Concerns
- **Overlap Resolution Edge Cases**: At extremely high particle counts ($N > 150$) and high temperatures, the naive elastic collision and overlap resolution step can occasionally result in particles clipping boundaries or overlapping briefly before separating.
- **Adiabatic Volume Limits**: While $V_C$ and $V_D$ are computed analytically from $T_H, T_C, V_A, V_B$, any manual slider volume override during a running Carnot cycle can disrupt FSM transitions. The code successfully locks standard controls during active cycle runs to mitigate this.

## Recommendations
1. Keep the maximum particle count slider capped at $150$ to preserve 60fps rendering and collision stability.
2. Consider adding an option in future sprints for users to toggle particle rendering entirely in piston engine mode to let them focus purely on the glowing gas color field.
