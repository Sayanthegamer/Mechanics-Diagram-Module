## Lessons Learned
- **Micro-to-Macro Coupling**: Directly scaling velocities by macroscopic temperature targets via Verlet step scaling is highly effective for visual simulators, but speed clamping is essential to prevent numerical explosion during rapid compression.
- **Bi-disperse representation**: Utilizing heavy (Red, mass 4.0) and light (Blue, mass 1.0) particles provides immediate intuitive visual proof of velocity distributions and diffusion separation.
- **Diagram Utility**: Introducing the Temperature-Entropy ($T$-S) diagram alongside the Pressure-Volume ($PV$) diagram dramatically enhances the educational clarity of the Carnot cycle, as the T-S rectangular bounds represent idealized transitions perfectly.
