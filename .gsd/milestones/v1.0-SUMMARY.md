## Lessons Learned
- **3D Projection Inversion**: Dragging 3D vectors on a 2D screen requires a closed-form inverse mapping assuming a plane restriction (e.g. $Z=0$). This was successfully handled by solving the isometric projection:
  $$y_{3d} = \frac{dx + dy}{2}, \quad x_{3d} = \frac{dy - dx}{2}$$
- **Compiler/Closure Scopes**: In TypeScript callbacks (such as sidebar slider updates), variable type-narrowing can be lost. Capturing sub-properties (e.g., `const circ = config.circular`) locally resolves compiler errors cleanly.
- **Physical Bounds Constraints**: Standard FBD layouts often suffer from block clipping at steep incline angles. Offsetting the block center dynamically perpendicular to the incline wedge angle:
  $$\vec{r}_{offset} = \vec{r} + \hat{n} \cdot \frac{h}{2}$$
  is necessary to maintain perfect visual contact without clipping.
