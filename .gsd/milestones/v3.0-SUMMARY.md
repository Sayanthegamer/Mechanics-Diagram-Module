## Lessons Learned
- **Integration Stability**: Simulating orbits requires numerical solvers to be selected carefully. RK4 works exceptionally well for open trajectories (Escape Velocity launcher), while Verlet or simple Euler-Cromer is more suited for closed/periodic loops to prevent energy drift.
- **Plummer Softening**: Using a small softening factor ($\epsilon = 0.15$) in Two-body simulations prevents singularities/extreme force blow-ups during close approaches without distorting the visual orbit shapes.
- **Generic Graphing**: Abstracting the coordinate and energy history structures into a common generic contract (`EnergyStatePoint`) enables multiple physics systems to utilize the exact same dynamic graph renderer seamlessly.
